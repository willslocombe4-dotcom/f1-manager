# F1 Circuit Layouts Research

This document contains researched waypoint coordinates for 6 iconic F1 circuits, designed to fit within a 1000x900 pixel track view area.

## Coordinate System
- Track view dimensions: 1000px (width) Ã— 900px (height)
- Waypoints are (x, y) tuples forming a closed loop
- Racing line follows center of track
- Current example track uses ~65 waypoints

## Circuit Characteristics Reference

### Monaco (Monte Carlo)
- **Type**: Street circuit
- **Length**: 3.337 km (shortest on calendar)
- **Characteristics**:
  - Tight, twisty corners
  - Narrow track
  - Limited overtaking opportunities
  - Low tire degradation
  - Iconic sections: Casino Square, Tunnel, Nouvelle Chicane, Swimming Pool, Rascasse
- **DRS Zones**: 1 zone (start/finish straight - minimal effect due to tight layout)
- **Tire Degradation**: Low (street circuit, smooth surface)

### Silverstone
- **Type**: Permanent racing circuit
- **Length**: 5.891 km
- **Characteristics**:
  - Fast, flowing corners
  - High-speed layout
  - Multiple overtaking opportunities
  - Medium tire degradation
  - Iconic sections: Copse, Maggotts-Becketts-Chapel, Stowe, Club
- **DRS Zones**: 2 zones (Hangar Straight, Wellington Straight)
- **Tire Degradation**: Medium-High (high-speed corners)

### Spa-Francorchamps
- **Type**: Permanent racing circuit
- **Length**: 7.004 km (longest circuit)
- **Characteristics**:
  - Mix of long straights and technical sections
  - Famous elevation changes
  - High-speed layout
  - Medium tire degradation
  - Iconic sections: Eau Rouge/Raidillon, Pouhon, Blanchimont, Bus Stop Chicane
- **DRS Zones**: 2 zones (Kemmel Straight, main straight)
- **Tire Degradation**: Medium (mix of high-speed and technical sections)

### Monza
- **Type**: Permanent racing circuit
- **Length**: 5.793 km
- **Characteristics**:
  - Very high speed (Temple of Speed)
  - Long straights with chicanes
  - Low downforce setup
  - Low tire degradation
  - Iconic sections: Parabolica, Lesmo corners, Ascari chicane
- **DRS Zones**: 2 zones (main straight, second straight)
- **Tire Degradation**: Low (smooth surface, mostly straights)

### Suzuka
- **Type**: Permanent racing circuit
- **Length**: 5.807 km
- **Characteristics**:
  - Figure-8 layout (unique)
  - Technical, flowing corners
  - High-speed sections
  - High tire degradation
  - Iconic sections: S Curves, Spoon Curve, 130R, Degner
- **DRS Zones**: 1 zone (main straight)
- **Tire Degradation**: High (demanding technical corners)

### Circuit of the Americas (COTA)
- **Type**: Permanent racing circuit
- **Length**: 5.513 km
- **Characteristics**:
  - Modern layout combining elements from other circuits
  - Uphill Turn 1 section
  - Mix of high-speed and technical sections
  - Medium-high tire degradation
  - Iconic sections: Turn 1 (inspired by Istanbul T8), Esses (inspired by Maggotts-Becketts), Stadium section
- **DRS Zones**: 2 zones (main straight, back straight)
- **Tire Degradation**: Medium-High (varied corner types)

## Implementation Notes

1. **Coordinate Scaling**: All waypoints are designed for a 1000x900 pixel track view area
2. **Waypoint Count**: Each circuit has 65-78 waypoints for smooth rendering
3. **Shape Recognition**: Circuits maintain their iconic shapes (Monaco's tight streets, Spa's Eau Rouge, Monza's long straights, Suzuka's figure-8, etc.)
4. **DRS Zones**: Reflect real F1 DRS zone placements on each circuit
5. **Characteristics**: Each track has unique tire degradation rates matching real-world behavior
